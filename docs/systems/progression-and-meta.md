# Progression & Meta

## In-Run Progression

Progression within a [run](../glossary.md#run) is driven entirely by [card level](../glossary.md#card-level). There is no experience gain, character leveling, or skill learning. The deck becomes more powerful only by acquiring higher-level cards.

Cards inherit their level from the [area level](../glossary.md#area-level) of the zone where they drop. To get stronger cards, the player must push into higher-level areas of the world. This creates a natural risk-reward loop: harder areas drop more powerful cards, but the enemies are proportionally tougher.

Loot from [encounters](../glossary.md#encounter) goes into the shared [bag](../glossary.md#bag). The party then organizes it into individual [decks](../glossary.md#deck) at [rest sites](../glossary.md#rest-site). Over time, the party replaces low-level cards with higher-level ones found in more dangerous areas.

## Meta Progression

Meta progression — rewards and unlocks that persist across runs — is TBD. See [progression-and-meta_tbd.md](progression-and-meta_tbd.md).

On [party wipe](../glossary.md#party-wipe), the party reverts to the last [rest site](../glossary.md#rest-site) save. Cards and loot found since that save are lost — the card levels were a property of those cards and are irrelevant once the cards are gone. The player resumes with whatever deck state they had at the last rest.
