# World

The current state of the world, roughly 100 years after [The Ruin](the-ruin.md). Civilization as it existed is gone. What remains is a patchwork of survivor settlements, contested territories, and vast stretches of dangerous, largely uninhabited land.

## Terrain Types

The landscape is shaped by the physical scars left behind by [The Ruin](the-ruin.md):

- **Urban Ruins** — collapsed cities that were never rebuilt, serving as scavenging grounds and danger zones
- **Toxic Wastes** — irradiated or chemically contaminated regions, impassable or deadly without preparation
- **The Overgrowth** — areas where nature has fully reclaimed the land over a century of abandonment
- **Underground Networks** — bunkers, tunnels, and subway systems that became refuges and now serve as settlements or lairs
- **Remnant Machine Zones** — areas still patrolled or controlled by automated pre-Ruin defense and infrastructure systems with no one left to shut them down

## Settlements

Survivor communities range from heavily fortified towns to loose camps. Most are small, isolated, and defensive. Some are under [faction](../glossary.md#faction) control; others try to remain independent. Settlements serve as safe zones, trading posts, and sources of [companions](../glossary.md#companion) and information during a run — TBD specifics.

Settlements also function as [rest sites](../glossary.md#rest-site) — the player can save progress and use settlement services between [encounters](../glossary.md#encounter).

## Threats

The world is actively dangerous outside of settlements:

- **Raiders** — Warborn-aligned or independent hostile groups preying on travelers and settlements
- **Mutants** — creatures and humans warped by the aftermath of The Ruin, concentrated in toxic and overgrowth zones
- **Remnant Machines** — pre-Ruin automated systems still executing old directives
