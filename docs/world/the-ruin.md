# The Ruin

## What It Was

The Ruin is the name survivors have given to the catastrophic collapse of the old world that occurred roughly 100 years before the events of the game. Civilization — governments, infrastructure, supply chains, communications — failed in a cascade that killed the vast majority of the global population within a generation.

No one alive today witnessed it. What people know comes from fragments: half-destroyed records, oral histories passed down through survivor communities, and the physical evidence left behind in the landscape.

## The Cause

The exact cause is — TBD. Possible options:

- **Global nuclear exchange** — deliberate or accidental, leaving irradiated zones and Remnant Machines still following old war directives
- **Engineered plague** — a biological weapon or lab accident that spread faster than any response could contain
- **Cascading environmental collapse** — resource depletion, climate failure, and infrastructure decay crossing a point of no return together
- **A combination** — the likeliest framing: no single cause, but several compounding failures that each made the others worse

The deliberate ambiguity of the cause is intentional. Different [factions](../glossary.md#faction) have conflicting narratives about what happened, and those narratives shape their identities and agendas.

## How It Shaped the World

The physical and social landscape of the current world is a direct consequence of The Ruin. See [World](world.md) for the current state of the game world.

## Faction Narratives

Each [faction](../glossary.md#faction) has a different interpretation of The Ruin that defines their identity and agenda. See [Factions](factions.md) for details.

The mentor's journal — passed to the [player character](../characters/player-character.md) at the start of the game — contains references to The Ruin that suggest the mentor knew more than most.
