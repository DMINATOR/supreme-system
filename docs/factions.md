# Factions

Several factions compete for control of resources and territory across the [wasteland](glossary.md#wasteland). They may influence available [encounters](glossary.md#encounter), recruitable [companions](glossary.md#companion), and [loot](glossary.md#loot) — TBD.

---

## The Remnants

Survivors dedicated to preserving and restoring pre-Ruin knowledge, laws, and structure. They believe civilization collapsed due to a failure of governance and that rebuilding the old order is the only path to long-term survival. They maintain archives, enforce old-world legal codes, and distrust those who have fully adapted to wasteland life.

## The Scavengers

Pragmatic wasteland traders and looters with no political agenda. They care little about the cause of [The Ruin](the-ruin.md) — the past is dead, only what you can carry matters. They move between settlements, trade across faction lines, and survive on opportunism. The most neutral faction.

## The Zealots

A cult that has built an entire religion around [The Ruin](the-ruin.md) and its aftermath. They treat the collapse as a divine or inevitable purge and believe the wasteland is a test or punishment for humanity's sins. Their worldview makes them dangerous and unpredictable — some chapters are penitent and reclusive, others are violently missionary.

## The Warborn

Raiders and warlords who have turned violence into a way of life. They see [The Ruin](the-ruin.md) as proof that strength is the only truth — everything else collapsed, and they didn't. Territory, tribute, and dominance define their culture. The most direct and persistent threat to isolated settlements.

## The Ghosts

A secretive, cell-based organization that claims to know the true cause of [The Ruin](the-ruin.md) — and has been suppressing that information for a century. They have no territory and no visible presence in the [wasteland](glossary.md#wasteland). Possibly descended from pre-Ruin intelligence agencies or corporate interests that survived by going underground. Most people don't believe they exist.

They only surface when someone gets too close to the truth. The mentor's journal appears to reference them — whether as allies, pursuers, or something else is — TBD.
